About

This is an online Rubik's cube puzzle simulator, where you can scramble the cube and try to solve it, or you can learn to solve the cube.

Standard Notation

This is how the moves of a Rubik's cube solution are displayed.
They consist of Letters, along with some single-quotes(') and in some cases, numbers.

Any letter as it is, denotes a Clockwise rotation of a particular face(90 degrees)
Any letter, followed by a single-quote(') denotes an Anti-clockwise rotation of that face.
If the letter is prefixed with a number, it means that the step should be repeated that many times.
Slice rotations rotate the middle layer of the cube.
Whole cube rotations like X,etc rotate the whole cube about an axis, by 90 degrees.

For Example, L means rotate the Left face Clockwise, and L' means Anti-Clockwise
Here, Left face refers to the face on the left, with respect to the front face. The front face is the one that you're facing.

2U' means, turn the upper face Anti-Clockwise, 2 times (i.e 180 degees).
M means, rotate the meridian of front face by 90 degrees, just like L.
E means, rotate the equator of front face by 90 degrees, just like D.
S means, rotate the middle slice by 90 degrees, just like F.

The Simulator

Here, you can:
1. move the pieces by clicking on any corner,edge,or outside the cube and dragging it in any direction.
3. click on the scramble button to scramble the Rubik's cube.
4. enter a list of moves like: R' D' R D, in the input box and click submit
5. click the solve button to solve the cube, and display the sequence of steps required.
You can see previous move, next move,the beginning and end stages of the cube by clicking on the required buttons

The Solver

If You click the solve button, the cube is solved, and the solution is displayed.
It is solved on a layer-by-layer basis, starting with the bottom layer(layer1), and ending with the top layer(layer3).
The Solution is displayed as a list of moves, each for solving its corresponding layer.
In the solution, 'initial' is the state of the cube prior to solving that layer.
You can click the next/previous buttons to move the highlight from one move to another.
The highlight represents the state of the cube after doing the current move
For example, if the solution for layer1 is:
initial R' D
and the highlighted move is R', it means that the cube shown, is after doing R' to the scrambled cube.
Sometimes, for an easy scramble, the solution would be too big.This is because it blindly follows the layer-by-layer approach of solving the cube.
